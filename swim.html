SWIM >>

Swimming is one of the best cardio workouts for your body because it allows you to elevate your heart rate while working numerous different muscles of your body. Lap pool swimming is a lot different than leisure swimming, so it’s a good idea to have a plan of what your workout is going to be before you dive in.

Materials:

  • Lap Pool
  • Bathing Suit
  • Goggles
  • Swim Cap
  • Timer

Steps:

  1. START with a warm up
    • Multiply the number of lengths of the pool you want to swim by 25 yards to calculate how many yards your warm up will be.
    • The goal is to get your muscles warmed up and your breathing heavier to prepare your body for the workout.
  2. BEGIN your workout
    • Make sure you incorporate all four strokes including, butterfly, backstroke, breaststroke, and freestyle.
    • Almost all swim workouts include timed intervals so make sure these intervals push your body hard enough to get a strong workout, but also allow your muscles to rest in between sets.
  3. END with a cool down
    • This part of your workout is crucial because your muscles need time to cool off and gradually lower your heart rate.
    • Make your cool down around the same amount of laps you completed during your warm up.

Additional Tips:

  • Using goggles will improve your vision underneath the water.
  • Wearing a swim cap will not only prevent your hair from getting damaged by the strong chemicals, it will also allow you to swim faster by pulling back any hairs that could break the water current.
  • When planning out your workout, you want to make sure each set includes time intervals. Check the timer in between sets to stay on track of the time.
